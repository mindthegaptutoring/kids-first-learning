Questions parents usually ask

How long should my child's session be?

25 minutes is great for younger kids or those just starting reading support. It targets key skills and builds confidence without losing pace — and it's an excellent length for children with ADHD or a need to move.

55 minutes is best for children who can sustain focus and are ready for more: additional practice, games, and extended reading activities.

How often should we book?

Two sessions per week is the recommended minimum for children who are struggling. Many students come 3–4 times weekly because consistent practice is what makes skills take hold between lessons. That said, I completely understand this isn't always possible — progress will still happen, and we'll make the most of every session.

Is online tutoring as effective as in-person?

For most students, yes. Sessions are live and interactive with screen sharing — the dynamic is very similar to sitting together. Most families who are skeptical about this come around after the first session.

Does this work for homeschool families?

Absolutely — and as a homeschool mom myself, I understand what flexibility actually means for a family. Sessions adapt to any curriculum or no curriculum at all, and scheduling works around your rhythm, not a school calendar.
