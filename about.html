About Heather

Hi, I'm Heather.

Teaching children to read has always been personal for me. Long before I had my own classroom, I watched my brother struggle with reading every day. Homework was hard. School was frustrating. Failing was embarrassing, and over time, his confidence suffered. My parents tried everything they could — they just didn't always have the right tools. I remember how hard that was for all of them.

It's why I care so deeply about helping children not just learn to read, but feel confident and capable while doing it. Whether I'm working with your child or my own, the goal stays the same: learning should be fun, meaningful, and something your child can succeed at and feel proud of.

I have a Bachelor's degree, a K–12 teaching certificate, and training in the Science of Reading. Over the past 26 years, I've taught in public schools, tutoring settings, online classes, and homeschooled my own children. Those experiences have let me support a wide range of learners — including those who feel stuck or overwhelmed — and I've had the joy of watching them grow into confident readers who say, "I can read."

In my sessions, I keep learning interactive through games, discussion, and guided practice. Each lesson is built around your child's specific needs — not a generic curriculum. I use explicit phonics instruction, multi-sensory techniques, movement, and spiral review to make learning feel manageable rather than overwhelming.

As a homeschool mom, I understand what flexibility actually looks like for families. I'm happy to support what you're already doing at home, whether your child needs extra help, more practice, or a challenge.

A little about me outside of teaching: I'm a mom of two, and we have two cats and a dog at home. I love reading, baking, playing games, and staying active. It would be an honour to work with your child.

What to expect in sessions

Starts with a diagnostic

The first session is about finding exactly where your child is — not assuming. That shapes everything we do from there.

Built around your student

No generic curriculum. Sessions are tailored to the specific gaps showing up and adapt as those gaps close.

Progress, not perfection

Mistakes are part of learning. Sessions are a safe space where kids feel comfortable trying — and proud of the effort they're putting in.

Honest feedback

After every session, you know what we worked on, what progress was made, and what comes next.

Works with any curriculum

Whether you homeschool with a structured program or a relaxed approach, sessions fit around what you're already doing.

No contracts

Book as much or as little as your family needs. The goal is independence — not ongoing dependency on tutoring.
